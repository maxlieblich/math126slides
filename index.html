    Instructions

    These slides should work with any modern browser: IE 9+, Safari 5+, Firefox 9+, Chrome 16+.

    • Choose a lecture from the dropdown. The lecture slides are designed to be viewed in fullscreen mode (F11 on most browsers). The button takes you to a printable version. The button opens a video of the current lecture segment.
    • The dropdown allows you to set "Reading mode" or "Presentation mode". In presentation mode, you can step through the lecture the way it happens in class. Some slides are revealed in steps. In reading mode, each slide is fully visible right away.
    • Navigate by swiping or using arrow keys. You may need to give the window focus by clicking outside the lecture frame for key commands described throughout this slide to work properly.
    • Press M to see a menu of slides. Press G to go to a specific slide.
    • When interacting with 3D objects, use left click, middle click, right click or hold A, S, D on the keyboard and move the mouse to rotate, scale, or pan the object.

    If your browser or hardware does not support WebGL, interacting with models will be very slow (and in general models can get CPU-intensive). Navigate to a slide away from any running model to stop model animation.
